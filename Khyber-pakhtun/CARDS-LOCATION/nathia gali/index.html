Nathia Gali

Introduction

Nathia Gali is a charming hill station located in the Abbottabad District of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. Famous for its cool climate, lush green scenery, and colonial-era charm, it’s a popular retreat from the summer heat.

Historical Background

During British colonial rule, Nathia Gali served as a summer headquarters for civil servants. Its old bungalows and narrow winding roads still reflect the architectural style of that era.

Geography and Features

  • Elevation: Around 2,410 meters above sea level
  • Climate: Cool summers and snowy winters
  • Trails: Scenic hiking paths like Mukshpuri and Miranjani

Flora and Fauna

Nathia Gali is part of the Ayubia National Park, making it rich in biodiversity. You can spot monkeys, leopards, and a variety of birds, along with thick forests of pine, oak, and cedar.

Tourist Attractions

  • Nathia Gali Church
  • Mukshpuri Top & Miranjani Trek
  • Ayubia Chairlift (nearby)

Quick Facts

  • District: Abbottabad, KPK
  • Famous For: Cool climate, hiking, and colonial architecture
  • Best Time to Visit: May to September
  • Part of: Galiyat region
