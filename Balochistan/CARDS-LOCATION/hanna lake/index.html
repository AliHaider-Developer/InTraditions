Hanna Lake

Introduction

Hanna Lake is a beautiful artificial lake located near Quetta in Balochistan, Pakistan. Surrounded by rugged mountains and pine trees, the lake is a popular spot for picnics, boating, and nature photography.

Historical Background

Hanna Lake was constructed in 1894 during British colonial rule to collect and store water for Quetta. It was built by constructing a dam between two hills and has since served as both a reservoir and a tourist attraction.

Geography and Features

  • Location: About 14 km east of Quetta city in the Urak Valley.
  • Scenery: The turquoise lake reflects the surrounding mountains, creating a breathtaking landscape.
  • Boating: Visitors can enjoy boat rides across the calm waters during the tourist season.

Recreational Significance

Hanna Lake is a favorite leisure destination for locals and tourists alike. A restaurant and picnic areas nearby make it ideal for day trips, while the area is also popular among hikers and birdwatchers.

Preservation and Challenges

In times of drought, the lake has occasionally dried up, raising concerns about water conservation and environmental management. Local authorities continue efforts to maintain the lake’s ecological balance.

Quick Facts

  • Constructed: 1894
  • Location: Near Quetta, Balochistan, Pakistan
  • Features: Artificial lake, boat rides, picnic areas
  • Distance from Quetta: Approximately 14 km
  • Best Time to Visit: March to October
